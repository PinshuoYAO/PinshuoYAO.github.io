Development of High-throughput Lipid Binding Screening Platform

2023 - Present

This ongoing project focuses on developing a novel high-throughput platform for screening protein-lipid interactions using yeast display technology. The system allows for rapid evaluation of binding properties across thousands of protein variants simultaneously, providing insights into sequence determinants of lipid binding specificity.

Key Components:

  • Yeast Display System: Expression of protein variants on yeast cell surface for efficient screening
  • Fluorescence-based Detection: Quantitative measurement of binding affinities
  • AI Modeling Integration: Using machine learning to analyze binding patterns and predict optimal sequences
  • Structural Validation: Confirming computational predictions with experimental structures

The platform aims to accelerate the discovery and design of proteins with custom lipid-binding properties, with potential applications in drug delivery, biosensors, and synthetic biology.

Biophysical Properties of Sperm Chromosomes

2021 - 2023 (Master's Research)

During my Master's program, I investigated the unique biophysical properties of chromosomes in mammalian sperm cells. This research focused on understanding the specialized chromatin packaging mechanisms that enable extreme DNA compaction while maintaining the ability to rapidly decondense upon fertilization.

Research Highlights:

  • Chromatin Dynamics: Characterized the aggregation and disaggregation mechanisms of sperm chromatin
  • Protamine-DNA Interactions: Analyzed the role of protamine proteins in DNA compaction
  • Epigenetic Preservation: Examined how essential epigenetic marks are maintained despite extreme compaction
  • Fertilization Mechanics: Studied the rapid chromosome reorganization during early embryogenesis

This research provided insights into fundamental reproductive biology and contributed to our understanding of male fertility challenges and potential interventions.
