01 / About

A scientist between cities, languages, and disciplines.

I'm Yao Pinshuo. I grew up in Harbin, learned Japanese in Tokyo at sixteen, fell in love with biology at Tokyo University of Agriculture, and never stopped chasing molecules.

Today I am a PhD student at the Institute of Industrial Science, UTokyo, in the Tsuboyama Lab. My instruments are yeast displays, machine learning models, and BLI sensors. My questions: how does a protein know which lipid it loves, and can we teach a computer to design new ones from scratch?

Outside the lab you'll find me on a mountain trail, underwater with a regulator, or hunting for the perfect bowl of ramen.

02 / Research

Decoding the lipid alphabet, one protein at a time.

Two ongoing projects, one obsession: a universal grammar for protein-membrane recognition.

Two parallel tracks. One quantitative — measuring binding kinetics with rigour. One exploratory — letting machines find the patterns we can't see. Together they form one map of how proteins read membranes.

PROJ 01

Phospholipid binding landscape of peripheral membrane proteins

Yeast DisplayNGSML

High-throughput screening across thousands of variants, mapping which proteins bind which lipids and why.

~10⁵variants screened
8lipid classes

PROJ 02

A standardized BLI method for lipid-protein binding kinetics

BLIKineticsLiposomes

A reproducible bio-layer interferometry protocol so anyone, anywhere, can measure the same kₐ and k_d for the same complex.
