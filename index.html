About Me

I am a PhD student at The University of Tokyo, focusing on AI-assisted protein design. My research involves developing large-scale protein analysis methods to understand the amino acid code that defines protein function, enabling more efficient design of artificial proteins.

With a strong background in biology, I am committed to advancing applications in artificial protein design while addressing fundamental biological questions.

Learn more

Research

My primary research focus is on protein-lipid binding specificity. I aim to understand the molecular mechanisms governing how proteins recognize and interact with specific lipids, which is crucial for numerous cellular processes.
